History

The Treaty of Waitangi

Known as the founding document of New Zealand, the Treaty of Waitangi takes it name from the place where it was first signed. On February 6 1840, the document was signed and then began traveling around New Zealand. The purpose of the Treaty was to protect Māori while at the same time provide the Crown the ability to govern and represent the interests of New Zealand. The treaty exists in two versions, an English version and a Māori version. Some language differences between the two version has often been a point of debate through history.
